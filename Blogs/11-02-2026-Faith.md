**Author:** Faith Njekwe

## Health Scores Now match european Nutri-Scores

Prior to this update, our health score was based on a custom system that added or subtracted points using fixed penalties for sugar, salt, calories, and saturated fat, with bonuses for fibre and protein. While this worked reasonably well, it did not align with any recognised public health standard and could produce scores that didn’t match the Nutri-Score label consumers see on real packaging across Europe. To improve accuracy and consistency, I researched the official European Nutri-Score methodology and redesigned our scoring system to follow the same underlying principles. This involved categorising nutritional values into defined threshold “bins” and assigning points based on where each nutrient falls, rather than using arbitrary penalties.

The new system calculates negative points for energy, sugars, saturated fat, and sodium, and positive points for fibre, protein, and fruit/vegetable content, closely mirroring the Nutri-Score algorithm. I also implemented category-specific handling for beverages, fats, cheese, and fruits and vegetables, since Nutri-Score evaluates these differently due to their unique nutritional roles. These points are combined into a Nutri-Score–style total, which is then converted into our 0–100 health score scale so it remains intuitive for users while still reflecting the official grading system (A to E). This makes our scores more scientifically grounded, consistent with European standards, and more trustworthy for users comparing products.
